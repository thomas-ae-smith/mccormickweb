This was a building made up of two separate blocks linked only by access through two utility rooms on the ground floor and access through two bed rooms on the first floor. The courtyard created by the L shape of these blocks is south facing and has fine long views out across the Windrush Valley, but the existing configuration of the house limited the access between house and garden and did not address the view.

The primary design aim was to connect seamlessly both wings of the building. The secondary aim was to create a dynamic central core and the third was to provide accommodation that links inside and out and takes full advantage of the view.

A simple statement quarter-circle kitchen extension in the angle between the two blocks solves many of the original issues of circulation and orientation. The need for a corridor at ground level is avoided by re-locating the kitchen into the heart of the plan, while allowing the focus of the whole building to shift to address the private sunny face and views.
